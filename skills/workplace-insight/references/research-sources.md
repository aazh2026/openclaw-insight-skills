## Search Strategies

### Finding Specific Studies
```
"topic" "Journal of Applied Psychology" site:scholar.google.com
"topic" "Harvard Business Review" year:2020-2025
"topic" meta-analysis workplace
```

### Verifying Citation Quality
1. Check journal impact factor
2. Verify author affiliations (university/consulting firm)
3. Look for sample size and methodology
4. Check if findings have been replicated

### Red Flags to Avoid
- No author credentials listed
- No methodology section
- Sample size < 50 without justification
- Published in predatory journals
- Findings contradict established meta-analyses

## Citation Format

### Journal Article
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Journal*, Volume(Issue), page range. https://doi.org/xxxxx

### Research Report
Organization Name. (Year). *Title of report*. Publisher. URL

### Working Paper
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of paper. Working Paper No. XX, Institution.

## Effect Size Interpretation

### Common Metrics
- **Cohen's d**: 0.2=small, 0.5=medium, 0.8=large effect
- **r (correlation)**: 0.1=small, 0.3=medium, 0.5=large
- **R²**: Percentage of variance explained

### Reporting Standards
Always include:
- Effect size when available
- Sample size (n=XXX)
- Statistical significance (p < 0.05)
- Confidence intervals if reported
